# Past Continuous Tense

## What is the Past Continuous Tense?

The Past Continuous tense (sometimes called the **Past Progressive**) is used to describe actions that were ongoing or in progress at a specific time in the past. It is often used to set the scene for a story or to describe an action that was interrupted.

### Interrupted Actions

We use the Past Continuous to indicate that a longer action in the past was interrupted by a shorter action (usually in the Past Simple).

> "I was watching a movie when the power went out."

### Specific Time as an Interruption

We use it to describe an action that was happening at a specific exact moment in the past.

> "At 8 PM last night, we were eating dinner."

### Parallel Actions

We use it to describe two or more ongoing actions happening at the exact same time in the past.

> "I was studying while my brother was listening to music."

# Past Continuous Rules

Understanding how to form the Past Continuous tense correctly is essential for clear communication in English.

## Forming the Past Continuous

### Affirmative Sentences

**I / He / She / It + was + verb + -ing**\
Example: *"I was reading a book last night."*

**You / We / They + were + verb + -ing**\
Example: *"They were playing football yesterday."*

### Negative Sentences

**I / He / She / It + was not (wasn't) + verb + -ing**\
Example: *"She wasn't sleeping when I called."*

**You / We / They + were not (weren't) + verb + -ing**\
Example: *"We weren't waiting for the bus."*

### Questions

**Was + I / he / she / it + verb + -ing?**\
Example: *"Was he working late yesterday?"*

**Were + you / we / they + verb + -ing?**\
Example: *"Were you talking to him?"*

# Spelling Rules for Adding -ing

*Note: The spelling rules for the Past Continuous are the same as those used for the Present Continuous.*

| Rule | Example |
|---|---|
| Most verbs: add **-ing** | play → playing, eat → eating |
| Verbs ending in silent **-e**: drop **e**, add **-ing** | write → writing, make → making |
| Short verbs (consonant-vowel-consonant): double final consonant + **-ing** | run → running, sit → sitting |
| Verbs ending in **-ie**: change **ie → y** + **-ing** | lie → lying, die → dying |

## More Examples for Adding -ing

|---|---|---|

# Time Expressions with Past Continuous

## Specific Past Moments

-   At 8 o'clock yesterday
-   At midnight
-   This time last year
-   All day / All morning

## Interruption and Parallel Markers

-   **When** (usually followed by a Past Simple action)
-   **While** (usually followed by a Past Continuous action)
-   **As** (used similarly to "while" to show background actions or exact simultaneous timing)

# Examples & Usage

See the Past Continuous tense in action with these common examples.

## Interrupted Actions

### Affirmative

-   "I was taking a shower when the phone rang."
-   "They were walking in the park when it started to rain."
-   "She was cooking dinner when her guests arrived."

### Negative

-   "I wasn't sleeping when you knocked on the door."
-   "He wasn't paying attention when the teacher asked the question."

### Questions

-   "Were you driving when the accident happened?"
-   "What were you doing when the alarm went off?"
-   "Was it raining when you left the house?"

## Parallel Actions & Atmosphere

### Simultaneous Events

-   "While I was washing the dishes, my husband was drying them."
-   "The children were playing outside while the parents were talking inside."

### Setting the Scene in a Story

-   "The sun was shining, the birds were singing, and everyone was enjoying the beautiful weather."
